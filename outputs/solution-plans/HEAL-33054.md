# Solution Plan — HEAL-33054

## 1. Problem Statement

No mechanism existed to flag whether an Account belongs to a Cardone Ventures (CV) employee, and no corresponding visibility existed on Opportunity records. Business users (primarily the operations team, led by Ivelisse Santos and Ashlee Edwards) had no way to identify CV employee accounts in Salesforce or filter/report on related Opportunities by this attribute. This is a net-new feature request — no prior field, flag, or workaround existed in Production.

---

## 2. Escalation Decision

**Decision: Support-Resolvable**

Confidence: High.

This is a straightforward declarative Salesforce configuration task: one new checkbox field on Account, one new formula checkbox field on Opportunity referencing the Account field, FLS grants across relevant permission sets, and layout placement. No Apex, no Flow, no complex automation, and no Engineering involvement required. Sean Bingham completed the full build in Sandbox and raised PRs #9062 and #9065 via Gearset on 2026-05-12.

---

## 3. Proposed Fix (Minimal Viable)

### Why this fix and not a bigger one

The business need is simple: "mark an Account as a CV employee account and surface that flag on Opportunities for reporting." The minimal solution is:
- One editable checkbox on Account (source of truth).
- One read-only formula field on Opportunity that mirrors it (no independent override needed at this time).
- FLS and layout placement to make both fields visible to the right users.

Alternatives considered and rejected:
- **Custom object or relationship:** Unnecessary — this is a boolean attribute of an Account, not a separate entity.
- **Opportunity-level editable checkbox:** Would introduce data inconsistency risk (Account and Opportunity out of sync). A formula field is correct because the Opportunity value is derived from the Account — it should never be independently set.
- **Automation/Flow to copy the value:** Adds complexity and introduces sync lag or failure risk. A formula field is instantaneous, maintenance-free, and always accurate.
- **Validation rule to prevent unchecking:** Out of scope for the MVP. Business did not request this and it would restrict legitimate data correction. Noted as a future consideration.

### Exact Steps

**Step 1 — Account field (already created in Sandbox)**
- Object: Account
- Field Label: Cardone Ventures Employee
- API Name: `Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c`
- Type: Checkbox
- Default Value: Unchecked (false)
- Description: Indicates this Account belongs to a Cardone Ventures employee.

**Step 2 — Opportunity field (already created in Sandbox)**
- Object: Opportunity
- Field Label: Cardone Ventures Employee
- API Name: `Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c`
- Type: Formula (Checkbox)
- Formula: `Account.Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c`
- Description: Read-only. Reflects the CV Employee flag from the related Account.

**Step 3 — FLS (already configured in Sandbox)**
- Account `Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c`:
  - `Operations_Manager` permission set: Read + Edit
  - `Admin_Team`, `Medical_Coordinator`, `Nursing_Staff`, `Operations_Team`: Read-only
- Opportunity `Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c` (formula — inherently read-only):
  - All five permission sets above: Read access granted

**Step 4 — Layout placement (already configured in Sandbox)**
- Account layouts (6 total):
  - `Account-Account Layout`: Account Summary section, after `Do_Not_Sync_to_Biocanic__c`
  - 5 PersonAccount layouts: after `Brand__c` or `VIP_Account__c` as appropriate per layout
- Opportunity layouts (31 total):
  - All in-use layouts: Opportunity Information section, after `AccountId`, displayed as read-only

**Step 5 — Deploy to Production**
- Confirm PRs #9062 and #9065 are merged and deployed via Gearset.
- Validate in Production: open a sample Account record — confirm `Cardone Ventures Employee` checkbox is visible and editable (for Ops Manager). Open a related Opportunity — confirm the formula field is visible and read-only.

**Step 6 — Notify business and initiate backfill**
- Notify Ivelisse Santos and Ashlee Edwards that fields are live.
- Instruct team to manually identify CV employee Accounts and check `Cardone Ventures Employee` on each.
- No Opportunity-level backfill is needed — formula fields auto-reflect once the Account is updated.

## 5. Dependencies

- **Gearset PRs #9062 and #9065** must be merged and successfully deployed to Production before any user-facing validation can occur.
- **Business team backfill:** The formula field on Opportunity will show `false` for all existing records until the corresponding Account checkbox is checked. Accuracy of reporting depends entirely on the completeness of the manual Account-level backfill by Ivelisse Santos / Ashlee Edwards and their team.
- **No existing list of CV employee Accounts:** Ivelisse Santos confirmed (2026-05-12) there is no prior flag or data source to drive automated backfill. Manual identification is the only path.
- **Permission sets must be assigned correctly:** Users who need to edit the Account checkbox must have the `Operations_Manager` permission set assigned. Users who only need to view it must have one of the other four permission sets.
- **No changes to record types:** The Opportunity formula field is deployed across all 31 layouts covering all Opportunity record types — no record type filtering is needed.

### Sandbox Plan

### Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Incomplete backfill — CV employee Accounts missed | High | Medium | Business team must maintain a manual list; no automated fallback exists |
| Reporting shows incorrect `false` values during backfill window | High | Low-Medium | Expected transient state; communicate to business before go-live |
| Ops Manager accidentally unchecks Account checkbox after Opportunities exist | Low | Medium | Formula auto-updates; reporting changes silently. Future: consider validation rule or audit field |
| Gearset deployment conflict with in-flight layout changes | Low | Low | Review active PRs before merging; coordinate with team |
| Formula field returns blank if Account is not populated on Opportunity | Low | Low | Standard Salesforce behavior; no Opportunity should lack an Account in this org |

### Rollback Steps

If the deployment causes issues in Production:

1. **Remove fields from layouts first** (via Setup > Object Manager > Page Layouts) to prevent user-facing errors — do this before deleting fields.
2. **Revoke FLS** on all five permission sets for both fields.
3. **Delete `Opportunity.Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c`** (formula field — no data loss, safe to delete).
4. **Delete `Account.Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c`** — WARNING: if any Account records have been manually checked in Production during the backfill window, deleting this field will permanently destroy that data. Before deleting, export all Account records where `Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c = true` via Data Export or a SOQL query as a backup.
5. Raise a new Gearset PR to deploy the rollback metadata if needed.
6. Notify Ivelisse Santos and Ashlee Edwards that fields have been removed and the issue is under investigation.

**Minimal viable rollback:** If only the layout placement is problematic (e.g., field appears in wrong section), remove from layouts via Setup without touching the field or FLS — no data impact.
