# Solution Plan: HEAL-33066 — Price Book Creation

## 1. Problem Statement

Carlin French (reporter) requested the creation of two new Price Books in the Salesforce Production org — one for laboratory vendor **Quest** and one for **Evexia** — with pricing sourced from an external SharePoint spreadsheet. A secondary technical blocker was discovered: **59 Product2 records** referenced in the price books were marked inactive in Salesforce, preventing them from being added to any Price Book until reactivated. No end users could transact against these lab vendors using Salesforce pricing until both Price Books were created and populated.

## 2. Escalation Decision

**Support-Resolvable. No Engineering escalation required.**

This was a configuration and data task, not a break-fix defect. The work required:
- Direct data creation (Pricebook2 records, PricebookEntry records) via Salesforce UI/Setup
- A targeted data update (Product2 active flag) after receiving explicit business approval

No code, Apex, Flow logic, or deployable metadata was involved. The inactive product blocker was a data-state issue resolved through standard Setup operations with stakeholder sign-off.

## 3. Proposed Fix

**All steps are data-layer operations performed directly in the Production org. No metadata deployment required.**

## 5. Dependencies

- **Product2 activation prerequisite:** PricebookEntry records cannot be created for inactive products. The 59 product activations were a hard prerequisite for completing the Price Book entries. Business approval from Carlin was required before activation.
- **Standard Price Book baseline:** Salesforce requires all products to have a Standard Price Book entry before being added to a custom Price Book. This was confirmed handled during entry creation.
- **Spreadsheet accuracy:** All pricing data was sourced from `Lab Price Book Creation.xlsx` (reporter-owned SharePoint document). Pricing accuracy is dependent on that source being correct — any errors in the spreadsheet will surface during Carlin's team UAT.
- **Follow-up tickets expected:** Carlin's 2026-05-15 comment indicated additional adjustments are needed. Those changes should be captured in new tickets, not appended to HEAL-33066.

## 6. Sandbox Plan + Risk + Rollback

### Sandbox Plan
**Not applicable.** Price Books, Price Book Entries, and Product active/inactive status are data-layer records in Salesforce. They are not deployable metadata and cannot be moved between orgs via Gearset or the Metadata API. All changes were made directly in the Production org through the standard Salesforce UI, which is the correct and only supported method for this type of work.

If a Sandbox validation were needed for a similar future task, the approach would be:
- Replicate the same data entry steps in a Full or Partial Copy sandbox
- Confirm product activation has no unintended side effects on Orders, Opportunities, or CPQ rules
- Obtain sign-off before repeating in Production

### Rollback Steps

If the changes must be undone:

1. **Deactivate Price Books:** Set `IsActive = false` on the Evexia and Quest Pricebook2 records. This prevents them from being assigned to new Opportunities/Orders without deleting historical data.
2. **Delete PricebookEntry records:** Remove entries from both Price Books if full removal is required (requires no active Opportunities referencing them).
3. **Deactivate reactivated products (if needed):** If any of the 59 products prove to have been inactive for a valid reason, set `IsActive = false` on those specific Product2 records. Requires business confirmation of which products to revert.
4. **No metadata rollback needed:** Since no deployable metadata was changed, there is nothing to revert in Gearset or via the Metadata API.

**Note:** Rollback is low-risk because all changes were additive. No existing records, rules, or automation were altered.
