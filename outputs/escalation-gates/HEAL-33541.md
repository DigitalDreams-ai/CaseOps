# Escalation Gate Decision — HEAL-33541

## Decision
Engineering-Required

## Confidence
Medium — 65% — The primary error originates from the Wellvi API and is most likely a vendor-side account misconfiguration, which would be vendor-resolvable without Engineering. However, the secondary character-count rejection (field reduced to 103 chars, below the stated 125-char limit, yet still failing) strongly suggests the Salesforce callout is constructing or truncating the payload incorrectly — a code-level defect that Support cannot fix. Additionally, Hypothesis 4 (the integration logic may be applying a blanket CA restriction in the callout, not just Tirzepatide) cannot be ruled out without Engineering reading the Apex code. Either of these pathways requires Engineering involvement regardless of the vendor outcome. Confidence is Medium rather than High because the vendor track has not been exhausted, and it is possible Wellvi resolves both issues entirely on their side.

## Key Evidence
- The Wellvi API returns `UnprocessableEntity: STATE_RESTRICTED` for a CA/TESA order — the reporter asserts CA is only restricted for Tirzepatide, meaning either Wellvi's account config is wrong OR the Salesforce callout is sending an incorrect product identifier that Wellvi maps to a restricted product. The second scenario is a code defect.
- The character-count rejection persisted even after Kaci reduced the field to 103 characters, below the previously accepted 125-character threshold — this is anomalous behavior that points to incorrect field construction or truncation logic in the Apex callout, not a user data entry error.
- No Apex callout code, Custom Metadata Types, Custom Settings, or Named Credentials have been retrieved or inspected — the integration's routing logic (including whether CA restriction is product-type-conditional or a blanket rule) is entirely unknown and cannot be assessed by Support.
- Hypothesis 4 from the investigation explicitly identifies that the Salesforce integration code may apply a blanket CA restriction that should only apply to Tirzepatide — this is a code-path concern that only Engineering can confirm or eliminate by reading the callout class.
- Two compounding and likely independent errors (STATE_RESTRICTED + character-count) means even if the vendor resolves the routing restriction, a second unresolved bug in the payload construction would continue to block submission — requiring an Engineering fix regardless.

## Red Flags
- The vendor track (Danielle Cress via Wellvi Slack) has not yet produced a response as of 2026-05-11. If Wellvi confirms their account config is incorrect and corrects it — AND the character-count issue also resolves — the Engineering escalation may become unnecessary. A human reviewer should confirm the vendor response status before Engineering time is committed.
- The product on the opportunity (TESA vs. Tirzepatide) has not been independently verified from the Opportunity record — if the product is actually Tirzepatide, the STATE_RESTRICTED error is correct behavior and the entire framing of the issue changes.
- Broader impact is unquantified — other CA + TESA Opportunities may be blocked by the same logic defect. Engineering scoping should include a query for similarly affected records before the fix is scoped as single-record.
