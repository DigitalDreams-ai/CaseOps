## Executive Finding

The root issue is not a global Customer Experience Case threading failure and not a confirmed missing Cx Email-to-Case route.

Production evidence shows Email-to-Case threading works for Customer Experience cases when the reply is delivered to a Salesforce-routed mailbox such as `support@10xhealthsystem.com` or `info@10xhealthsystem.com`. The affected example fails because the internal approval email was sent from/presented as an agent mailbox and the Outlook reply was addressed back to human/shared Outlook recipients, not to a Salesforce Email-to-Case routing address. Since Salesforce never receives that inbound reply, it cannot create an incoming `EmailMessage` even though the subject contains a valid thread token.

## Email Routing Configuration Observed

Production contains two distinct email mechanisms that previous notes conflated:

1. Email-to-Case routing addresses under the Case email service:
   - `patientdocs`
   - `support`
   - `tech-dataopps`
2. Per-user Email-to-Salesforce service addresses:
   - Many `emailtosalesforce` addresses using `*.le.salesforce.com`
   - These are authorized to individual senders and are not the same as Case routing addresses.

The 38 active `EmailServicesAddress` records are therefore not 38 active Email-to-Case routing addresses. Most are Email-to-Salesforce addresses.

## Root Cause

Replies to internal approval emails are not guaranteed to be delivered back to Salesforce. The affected workflow sends or exposes the email as an agent-to-internal-recipient conversation. Outlook replies go back to the agent and/or shared Outlook mailbox, bypassing the Salesforce Email-to-Case inbound address. A thread token in the subject/body is necessary for matching, but it is not sufficient; Salesforce must receive the email.

## Smoking Gun

The affected internal approval email used the standard Case email action on the Customer Experience Case layout:

- Layout: `Case-Customer Experience`
- Action: `Case.SendEmail`
- Label: `Email`
- Type: `SendEmail`
- Target object: `EmailMessage`
- Target parent field: `Parent`
- Exposed sender field: `ValidatedFromAddress`
- `ValidatedFromAddress` behavior: editable, with no predefined field override
- Only predefined field override: `ToIds = Case.ContactId`
- No Reply-To field on the action
- No enforced default From of `support@10xhealthsystem.com`, `info@10xhealthsystem.com`, or `refunds@10xhealthsystem.com`

The actual affected `EmailMessage` confirms the sender and template state:

- `EmailTemplateId = null`
- `EmailRoutingAddressId = null`
- `FromAddress = ntorres@10xhealthsystem.com`
- `ValidatedFromAddress = ntorres@10xhealthsystem.com`
- `ToAddress = tjones@10xhealthsystem.com`
- `BccAddress = ntorres@10xhealthsystem.com`
- `Headers = null`

Therefore the exact source of the problem is the Customer Experience Case `Case.SendEmail` action being used for internal approvals without enforcing a Salesforce-routed From/Reply-To address. The user sender was selected/defaulted to Natasha Torres's personal email address.

## Likely Failure Point

The failure is in the reply path for Cx/internal approval emails:

- The Customer Experience Case layout exposes `Case.SendEmail` with editable `ValidatedFromAddress`.
- The affected approval was sent with `ValidatedFromAddress = ntorres@10xhealthsystem.com`.
- There is no `EmailTemplateId` and no `EmailRoutingAddressId` on the message.
- The process relies on the agent's personal sender/Bcc path, which can log the outbound email but does not make future Outlook replies visible to the Case unless the reply is also delivered to Salesforce.

## Corrective Direction

Do not treat this as a simple "create a Cx routing address" fix. That would not capture replies addressed only to the agent.

Engineering/Admin should correct the internal approval reply path so any reply that must be part of the Case audit trail is delivered to Salesforce:

1. Configure Cx/internal approval Case emails to use a Salesforce-routed address as the visible Reply-To or From path, such as the existing supported Customer Experience mailbox.
2. If a shared refund/approval mailbox is required, configure that mailbox to forward thread-tokened replies into an Email-to-Case routing address.
3. Validate that Outlook replies from internal approvers create incoming `EmailMessage` records on the originating Case.
4. Avoid relying on per-user Email-to-Salesforce or Bcc-only behavior for case conversation capture.
