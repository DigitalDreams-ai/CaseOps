# Jira Message Draft — HEAL-32826

**CUSTOMER-FACING — POSTED TO JIRA**

Hi Danielle,

I investigated this issue. It requires an Engineering change and has been escalated.

**Problem:**

Between March 18 and March 27, the system that syncs Shopify orders to Salesforce stopped automatically creating Patient Agreements (Informed Consent 2026 records) when customers purchased MGT or GHT products. This meant several hundred patient accounts created during that window ended up in Salesforce without their consent records attached, even though the accounts themselves were created successfully.

**What needs to change:**

The Shopify order sync integration needs to be debugged and fixed. When an order syncs from Shopify to Salesforce, it should tell Salesforce to automatically create a Patient Agreement for that account. For a 9-day window, that automation broke. A fix was deployed on March 27 that appears to have resolved the issue for new orders going forward, but the accounts created during March 18-27 still need their missing consent records added back.

**Evidence:**

On March 27, something was fixed in the integration that restored the automation. New Shopify orders created after that date correctly show "Informed Consent 2026" on their accounts, which suggests the flow itself is working and the problem was with how Shopify orders were being synced during that specific window.

**Escalation notes:**

This is owned by the eCommerce and Shopify integration team (Adam Botma's group). They need to:
1. Confirm what changed on March 27 and whether it's already deployed to Production
2. Identify which part of the sync broke during March 18-27
3. Work with our team on the approach for backfilling the missing consent records for the affected accounts (several hundred PAM cases)

Thanks for providing the detailed scope and the reproduction steps. That made it clear this was a sync issue rather than a Salesforce automation problem.
