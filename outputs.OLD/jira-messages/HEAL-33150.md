Thank you for reporting this and providing the detailed Outlook screenshots. I investigated why the internal approval reply didn't attach to the case record.

## What We Found

When the approval email was sent through Salesforce, it used the agent's personal email address as the sender. When your colleague replied from Outlook, the response went back to that personal email inbox instead of to Salesforce. Since Salesforce never received the reply, it couldn't attach it to the case, even though the email subject contained the correct thread code.

This is different from replies sent to addresses like support@10xhealthsystem.com, which Salesforce monitors directly and logs automatically.

## What Happens Next

The reply routing for internal approval emails needs to be configured differently. I've escalated this to our engineering team to update how those emails are sent and where replies should be delivered. The solution will likely involve using a company-monitored Salesforce email address for the reply path, or setting up the approval mailbox to forward responses into Salesforce.

You'll hear from us once that work is complete and tested.

Thanks again for the screenshots and clear explanation. That detail made it easy to see exactly how the reply landed in Outlook and helped us identify the problem.
