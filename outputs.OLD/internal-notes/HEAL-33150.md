# Internal Notes - HEAL-33150

**Jira Issue:** HEAL-33150 - Cx Case Record Response  
**Status:** Escalated to Engineering  
**Reference:** Full investigation at `outputs/investigations/HEAL-33150.md`

## Root Cause (One-Line)

Internal approval email replies are not delivered to Salesforce because the Cx Case `SendEmail` action exposes an editable sender field with no default routing address; agent replies to personal email, not Salesforce-routed Email-to-Case.

## Decision

**Escalate to Engineering/Admin.** Production evidence confirms Cx threading works when replies reach routed mailboxes (support@, info@); the issue is the reply path, not record type. The fix requires forcing internal approval emails to use a Salesforce-routed From/Reply-To or forwarding the approval mailbox into Email-to-Case.

## Evidence Summary

- Affected message: `02sQl00000t0TfnIAE` (sender: `ntorres@10xhealthsystem.com`, no EmailTemplateId, no EmailRoutingAddressId)
- Case `500Ql00000ujeSTIAY`: outbound email exists, inbound approval reply does not
- Control: nearby Cx cases do log incoming replies when routed to support@/info@
- Root: Case.SendEmail on Case-Customer Experience layout has editable ValidatedFromAddress with no predefined override

## Production vs Sandbox

- Production: Read-only investigation only. No changes made.
- Sandbox: Not yet validated by Support. Engineering owns validation after updating reply routing.
- Deploy: Requires Production Setup/metadata/mailbox change after Sandbox sign-off.

## Handoff to Engineering

Do not frame as "Cx record type missing Email-to-Case route." A new route will not capture replies sent only to the agent. Engineering should configure:
1. Cx internal approval From/Reply-To to use a Salesforce-routed address, OR
2. Approval mailbox forwarding into Email-to-Case (preserve thread token), OR
3. Approval workflow moved to Salesforce-native approval process.

Then Sandbox-validate that internal approver Outlook reply creates an incoming EmailMessage on the originating Case.
