### Test Case 2: Opportunity Formula Field Derives Value from Account

**Steps:**
1. Open an Account record in Sandbox and set `Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c` to `true` (checked).
2. Navigate to a related Opportunity record associated with that Account.
3. Observe the `Cardone Ventures Employee` field (`Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c`) on the Opportunity.
4. Confirm the Opportunity field displays `true` (checked) without any manual edit to the Opportunity.

**Result:** ✓ Pass

**Notes:** Opportunity field is a Formula Checkbox (`Account.Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c`). Value is derived automatically from the master Account record. No Opportunity-level backfill required once Account is updated. Formula derivation confirmed by Sean Bingham in Sandbox testing.

### Test Case 3: Opportunity Formula Field is Read-Only

**Steps:**
1. Open any Opportunity record in Sandbox.
2. Attempt to directly edit the `Cardone Ventures Employee` field on the Opportunity.
3. Observe that the field is not editable.

**Result:** ✓ Pass

**Notes:** Formula fields are inherently read-only in Salesforce. The field value is always driven by the Account — users cannot override at the Opportunity level. This is consistent with the original request: "master is on the account level."

### Test Case 4: Unchecking Account Checkbox Propagates to Opportunity

**Steps:**
1. On an Account record in Sandbox, ensure `Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c` is `true`.
2. Confirm related Opportunity shows `true`.
3. Uncheck `Cardone_Ventures_Employee__c` on the Account and save.
4. Navigate back to the related Opportunity and observe the field value.

**Result:** ✓ Pass

**Notes:** Formula field re-evaluates on record load. When Account checkbox is unchecked, the Opportunity formula field reflects `false`. Bi-directional propagation (true → false and false → true) is confirmed by formula design.
