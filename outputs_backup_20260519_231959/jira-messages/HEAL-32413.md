Hi Jennifer,

I investigated this issue and can confirm it has been escalated to Engineering for a configuration change. Here is a full update on where things stand.

**Problem:**

When a Labs Opportunity reaches Final Approval in Salesforce, the platform locks the record and prevents any edits by users who are not admins or designated approvers. This is why you and the other nurses see the record-locked error when attempting to update the Lab Vendor field. This is not a permissions issue that can be resolved by changing a profile or permission set — it is driven by the Approval Process configuration itself (`finalApprovalRecordLock = true`).

**What needs to change:**

A new Labs-specific Approval Process will be created that does not lock the record upon final approval. This will allow nurses to update Lab Vendor (and Lab Name) on approved Labs Opportunities as part of the standard lab draw workflow. All other Opportunity types (non-Labs) will be unaffected — their approval lock behavior will remain exactly as it is today.

**Evidence:**

The root cause was confirmed by reviewing the Opportunity Approval Process configuration. The lock is coming from the approval process, not from a validation rule or flow (both of those were investigated and ruled out). Screenshots of the existing configuration and the proposed solution have been attached to this ticket.

**Escalation notes:**

This has been escalated to Engineering as Bug #20941 ("Bug: Nurses cannot update lab vendor on approved Labs Opps"). Engineering is reviewing the implementation. Once the fix is validated in Sandbox and deployed to Production, nurses will be able to update Lab Vendor on approved Labs Opportunities without any workaround needed.

I will continue to monitor and will update you as soon as Engineering confirms a deployment date. If you have additional affected records or urgent cases in the meantime, please let me know and we can explore interim options with the team.
