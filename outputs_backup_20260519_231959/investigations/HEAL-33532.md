## Salesforce Problem

### Confirmed Facts

- The SMS Magic integration is active in the Production Salesforce org for the 10X Health team.
- The standard SMS access bundle consists of three permission sets: **SMS App Permission Set**, **SMS Converse Conversation User**, and **SMS Magic Additional Permissions**.
- Angelica Del Toro, Rosemary Lawler, and Tiffany Ortiz already held all three permission sets in Production prior to this ticket — no changes were required for them.
- Alejandra Molina was missing: **SMS App Permission Set** (only).
- Meghan Langbehn was missing: **SMS App Permission Set**, **SMS Converse Conversation User**, and **SMS Magic Additional Permissions** (all three — the full bundle was absent).
- Sean Bingham assigned the missing permission sets directly in Production on 2026-05-11.
- No sandbox deployment was involved; this was a direct permission set assignment in Production.

### Hypotheses

- **Known cause (Alejandra Molina):** One of the three required SMS Magic permission sets ("SMS App Permission Set") was not assigned to her user record at the time of onboarding or when SMS access was provisioned for the team. Partial assignment left her unable to fully use SMS Magic.
- **Known cause (Meghan Langbehn):** None of the three required SMS Magic permission sets were assigned to her user record — she had no SMS Magic access at all. This suggests she was never provisioned for SMS access, possibly due to an onboarding gap or a missed step when the team was set up in Salesforce.
- **Why the other three were unaffected:** Angelica Del Toro, Rosemary Lawler, and Tiffany Ortiz had the complete bundle already, implying prior access was correctly provisioned for them.

### Why This Should Fix It

The SMS Magic integration gates access via Salesforce permission sets. Without the required permission sets, users cannot see or interact with the SMS Magic interface. Assigning the full standard bundle to both users restores their access to match the confirmed-working configuration held by the other three team members.

### Risks

- Low risk: permission set assignments in Salesforce are non-destructive and do not affect other users or org-wide settings.
- Minor risk: if Meghan Langbehn's license type does not support all three permission sets, assignment may silently fail or not grant full access — this would surface during user confirmation testing.
- No rollback complexity: permission sets can be removed from a user at any time if they were assigned in error.

### Rollback Plan

If needed, remove the newly assigned permission sets from either user via Setup → Users → [User] → Permission Set Assignments. This is a simple, immediate, non-destructive operation.
