## Issue Understanding

### Observed Behavior

When an agent sends an email from a Cx Case Record in Salesforce, the outbound email is delivered successfully to the recipient and includes the expected case thread number/token. However, when the recipient replies from their external mail client (Outlook), the reply is NOT routed back into Salesforce and is NOT logged against the case. As a result:
- Only the initial outbound email appears in the case Activity feed.
- Inbound replies are completely absent from the case record.
- The communication history is fragmented — subsequent outbound emails from Salesforce appear as disconnected new messages.
- There is no full paper trail of the back-and-forth conversation within the case.

This behavior appears specific to (or at least observed on) the **Cx Case Record** record type. It is unclear whether other Case record types in the org experience the same issue.

### Expected Behavior

- Email replies from external mail clients (e.g., Outlook) should be matched to the originating Salesforce case via the email thread token included in the original outbound email.
- Matched replies should be automatically ingested and logged as EmailMessage records under the correct case.
- All inbound and outbound messages should appear chronologically in the case Activity timeline.
- The behavior should work for both internal user responses and external client responses.

### Acceptance Criteria

1. When a recipient replies to a Salesforce case email from any external mail client (Outlook, Gmail, etc.), their reply is captured and logged as an EmailMessage under the originating Cx Case Record.
2. The reply appears in the case Activity timeline in proper chronological order.
3. The case thread token is correctly embedded in all outbound emails from Cx Case Record types and is preserved through reply chains.
4. The fix applies consistently across both internal (user-to-user) and external (agent-to-client) messaging scenarios on Cx Case Records.
5. No regression introduced for other Case record types.

### Hypotheses

1. **Missing or misconfigured Email-to-Case routing address for Cx Case Record type (Most Likely):** The outbound email from Cx Case Records may be using a `Reply-To` or `From` address that is not monitored by Email-to-Case. If the routing address is not active or not mapped to ingest emails for this case type, replies will never be pulled into Salesforce. The thread token alone is insufficient if the destination address is not an active Email-to-Case routing address.

2. **Thread token not preserved in Reply-To header:** The case thread token may be embedded in the email body but not in the `Reply-To` header. Outlook users replying may trigger a reply to the sender's address rather than the Email-to-Case routing address. If Outlook strips or overrides the routing address, the reply bypasses Email-to-Case ingestion entirely.

3. **Record type assignment on inbound email routing:** Email-to-Case routing rules may exist for other record types but not for the Cx Case Record type. Inbound replies may be rejected, ignored, or mis-assigned, failing to attach to the correct case.

4. **On-Demand Email-to-Case (SMTP forwarding) misconfiguration:** If the org uses On-Demand Email-to-Case, the email service address must be the actual `Reply-To` address in outbound emails. If the Cx Case Record type's email action uses a different sender/reply-to configuration, replies will not reach the Salesforce email service endpoint.

5. **Page layout or record type-specific email action override:** The Cx Case Record type may have a custom email action or a modified Send Email quick action that does not correctly insert the thread token or does not use the standard Email-to-Case reply-to address.

6. **Org-Wide Email Address used as sender:** If the outbound email is sent from an Org-Wide Email Address (OWA) rather than the Email-to-Case routing address, and the OWA is not set up to forward replies to the Email-to-Case service, replies will land in a mailbox not monitored by Salesforce.
